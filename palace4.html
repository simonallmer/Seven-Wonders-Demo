Palace — “Twin Palaces” (4-Player)

THE BOARD
Two Crystal Palaces are joined along their long edge into one field. Palace A (west) and Palace B (east) each keep their own five glass bands, heights 1–3, and their own elevators worked from their outer edge. Four seats hold the four ends — White (A-south) and Black (A-north) share Palace A; Red (B-south) and Blue (B-north) share Palace B. You play White; the rest are computer-led.

GOAL
Break every rival below four spheres (board + reserve). The last palace still standing wins.

THE SEAM
Because the palaces are attached, the old lethal side edge between them is now live terrain. Roll across the seam and the neighbour's band decides it: level ground rolls straight through, a step you can hop, a two-plus wall stops you dead, a drop sends you rolling down into their palace. You only fall — and shatter — off the two outer long edges.

ROLL
One action per turn: PLACE a sphere onto a free level-1 pane on your entrance row, or ROLL one sphere — a committed straight slide resolved by the terrain (flat keeps rolling, one-up hops if staged, two-plus is a wall, one-down keeps rolling, two-plus is a hard drop). Roll off an outer edge and the sphere is gone.

CAROM & SMASH
Roll into a sphere on the same tier to shove it one pane along — over an outer rim it dies. Drop onto any sphere and it is smashed and removed; you take its pane. Beware smashing your own.

ELEVATORS
A band answers to any seat standing a sphere on it — so roll into the enemy palace and you can seize their elevators. Raise a band by two to wall an attacker out at the seam; lower the ground to turn a rival's flat roll into a fatal drop off the far edge.
